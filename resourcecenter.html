Is a High Yield Savings Account Your Best Option?

High interest savings accounts are an ideal place to keep your emergency fund or any money to which still you need ready access. Your money will be safer than if you stuffed it under your mattress, and it will grow a bit, too. However, regardless of where you open a savings account, federal regulations limit you to six withdrawals or outgoing transfers each month. Exceed that number and you could be charged a fee. One notable exception is ATM withdrawals, which are not restricted.Remember, there are fewer ways to get your money from a savings account versus a checking account. You may not be able to write checks or get a debit card for your savings account -- both are perks typical of checking accounts. But if you opt for checking, you may get a very low interest rate, or none at all. Another common savings option is a CD, or certificate of deposit. CDs may earn a higher interest rate than savings accounts, particularly at brick-and-mortar banks. This may not be the case online, however, where you can find a number of high-interest savings options.
